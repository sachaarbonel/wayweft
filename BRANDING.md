# BRANDING.md

## Brand name
Wayweft

## Brand one-liner
Wayweft is a codebase intelligence layer for AI-assisted development.

## Positioning
Wayweft helps teams review changes after a Claude or Codex session, detect duplication and refactor drift, preserve codebase context, and carry knowledge forward across sessions.

## Brand attributes
- Calm
- Intelligent
- Durable
- Precise
- Premium
- Trustworthy

## What we are not
- Not a generic AI coding platform
- Not a flashy startup brand
- Not a security scanner
- Not a generic linting tool
- Not a playful mascot brand

## Core metaphor
Woven signal.

The brand should express continuity across sessions, agents, and code changes. The visual system should feel like threads being carried forward and woven into a coherent whole.

## Logo direction
Primary direction: Woven Signal

### Symbol
- A refined icon built from 2–3 interlaced lines
- The shape should subtly form a `W`
- Clean geometry, strong negative space
- No yarn, loom, or craft cliches
- No circuit-board or "AI sparkles" motifs

### Wordmark
- `Wayweft`
- Custom or carefully chosen typography
- Feels editorial, modern, and high-trust
- Prefer a refined serif or sharp humanist sans/serif pairing
- Avoid overly futuristic or playful type

### App icon
- Simplified woven `W`
- Legible at small sizes
- Works in one color and reversed color

## Visual tone
- Editorial, not gimmicky
- Technical, not cold
- Premium, not luxury-theatrical
- Geometric, not sterile
- Quietly distinctive

## Color palette
### Primary
- Deep Ink: `#1A1F35`
- Muted Teal: `#588184`
- Warm Brass: `#B59461`
- Paper: `#F3EBDC`

### Usage
- Deep Ink for primary text, logo, and key surfaces
- Muted Teal for secondary accents and calm emphasis
- Warm Brass for highlights, calls to attention, and premium contrast
- Paper for backgrounds and light surfaces

## Typography
### Recommended direction
- Headline: elegant serif or sharp humanist serif
- Body: restrained grotesk or neutral sans

### Typography traits
- High readability
- Moderate contrast
- No overly rounded startup fonts
- No techno/futurist display fonts
- No default "AI SaaS" look

## Layout principles
- Strong grid
- Generous whitespace
- Tight hierarchy
- Minimal ornament
- Use lines, paths, and layered structure as subtle motifs

## Motion direction
- Slow, deliberate, meaningful
- Thread-like convergence
- Layered reveal
- No bouncy micro-interactions
- No decorative animation for its own sake

## Illustration and graphic motifs
Use:
- woven paths
- layered traces
- route-like lines
- subtle map/grid structures
- structured overlays

Avoid:
- robots
- brains
- shields
- sparkles
- purple gradients
- generic code brackets as logo shapes

## Writing tone
- Clear
- Direct
- Thoughtful
- Technical without being stiff
- Confident without hype

## Naming and terminology
Prefer:
- codebase intelligence
- post-session review
- codebase memory
- handoff
- continuity
- review
- cleanup
- validation guidance

Avoid overusing:
- refactor scout
- AI-native
- autonomous
- magical
- revolutionary
- full coding platform

## Logo usage rules
- Keep clear space around the mark
- Do not distort or rotate the symbol
- Do not add glow or gradients by default
- Do not place on noisy backgrounds
- Ensure single-color version always works

## Favicon / small-size rule
If the full woven `W` loses clarity below 24px, use the simplified compact mark only.

## Docs/site design guidance
- Backgrounds should lean paper/light neutral, not pure white by default
- Use ink-heavy typography
- Brass should be sparing
- Teal should carry product/system accents
- Design should feel like a durable tool for serious engineering teams

## Canonical identity direction
Current preferred identity direction:
Woven Signal

This is the default for docs, website, logo exploration, and product visual design unless replaced by a later decision.
